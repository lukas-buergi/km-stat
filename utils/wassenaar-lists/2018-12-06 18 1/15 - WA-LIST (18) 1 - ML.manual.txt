ML19.	Directed Energy Weapon (DEW) systems, related or countermeasure equipment and test models, as follows, and specially designed components therefor:
ML19.a.	"Laser" systems specially designed for destruction or effecting mission-abort of a target;
ML19.b.	Particle beam systems capable of destruction or effecting mission-abort of a target;
ML19.c.	High power Radio-Frequency (RF) systems capable of destruction or effecting mission-abort of a target;
ML19.d.	Equipment specially designed for the detection or identification of, or defence against, systems specified by ML19.a. to ML19.c.;
ML19.e.	Physical test models for the systems, equipment and components, specified by ML19.
ML19.f.	"Laser" systems specially designed to cause permanent blindness to unenhanced vision, i.e., to the naked eye or to the eye with corrective eyesight devices.

ML19.Note 1.c.	High pulsed power or high average power radio frequency beam transmitters, which produce fields sufficiently intense to disable electronic circuitry at a distant target.

ML20.	Cryogenic and "superconductive" equipment, as follows, and specially designed components and accessories therefor:
ML20.a.	Equipment specially designed or configured to be installed in a vehicle for military ground, marine, airborne or space applications, capable of operating while in motion and of producing or maintaining temperatures below 103 K (‑ 170°C);
ML20.a.Note.	ML20.a. includes mobile systems incorporating or employing accessories or components manufactured from non-metallic or non-electrical conductive materials, such as plastics or epoxy-impregnated materials.
ML20.b.	"Superconductive" electrical equipment (rotating machinery or transformers) specially designed or configured to be installed in a vehicle for military ground, marine, airborne or space applications and capable of operating while in motion.
ML20.b.Note.	ML20.b. does not apply to direct-current hybrid homopolar generators that have single-pole normal metal armatures which rotate in a magnetic field produced by superconducting windings, provided those windings are the only superconducting components in the generator.
