
3.A.	SYSTEMS, EQUIPMENT AND COMPONENTS

3.A.Note 1.	The status of equipment and components described in 3.A., other than those described in 3.A.1.a.3. to 3.A.1.a.10., or 3.A.1.a.12. to 3.A.1.a.14., which are specially designed for or which have the same functional characteristics as other equipment is determined by the status of the other equipment.
3.A.Note 2.	The status of integrated circuits described in 3.A.1.a.3. to 3.A.1.a.9., or 3.A.1.a.12. to 3.A.1.a.14., which are unalterably programmed or designed for a specific function for another equipment is determined by the status of the other equipment.
3.A.Note 2.N.B.	When the manufacturer or applicant cannot determine the status of the other equipment, the status of the integrated circuits is determined in 3.A.1.a.3. to 3.A.1.a.9., and 3.A.1.a.12. to 3.A.1.a.14.
3.A.Note 3.	The status of wafers (finished or unfinished), in which the function has been determined, is to be evaluated against the parameters of 3.A.1.a., 3.A.1.b., 3.a.1.d., 3.A.1.e.4., 3.A.1.g., 3.A.1.h., or 3.A.1.i.

3.A.1.a.5.	Analogue-to-Digital Converter (ADC) and Digital-to-Analogue Converter (DAC) integrated circuits, as follows:
3.A.1.a.5.a.	ADCs having any of the following:
3.A.1.a.5.a.1.	A resolution of 8 bit or more, but less than 10 bit, with a "sample rate" greater than 1.3 Giga Samples Per Second (GSPS); 
3.A.1.a.5.a.2.	A resolution of 10 bit or more, but less than 12 bit, with a "sample rate" greater than 600 Mega Samples Per Second (MSPS); 
3.A.1.a.5.a.3.	A resolution of 12 bit or more, but less than 14 bit, with a "sample rate" greater than 400 MSPS;
3.A.1.a.5.a.4.	A resolution of 14 bit or more, but less than 16 bit, with a "sample rate" greater than 250 MSPS; or
3.A.1.a.5.a.5.	A resolution of 16 bit or more with a "sample rate" greater than 65 MSPS; 
3.A.1.a.5.a.N.B.	For integrated circuits that contain analogue-to-digital converters and store or process the digitized data, see 3.A.1.a.14.

3.A.1.a.5.a.Technical Notes.1.	A resolution of n bit corresponds to a quantisation of 2n levels.
3.A.1.a.5.a.Technical Notes.2.	The resolution of the ADC is the number of bits of the digital output that represents the measured analogue input.  Effective Number of Bits (ENOB) is not used to determine the resolution of the ADC.
3.A.1.a.5.a.Technical Notes.3.	For "multiple channel ADCs", the "sample rate" is not aggregated and the "sample rate" is the maximum rate of any single channel.
3.A.1.a.5.a.Technical Notes.4.	For "interleaved ADCs" or for "multiple channel ADCs" that are specified to have an interleaved mode of operation, the "sample rates" are aggregated and the "sample rate" is the maximum combined total rate of all of the interleaved channels.

3.A.1.a.5.b.	Digital-to-Analogue Converters (DAC) having any of the following:
3.A.1.a.5.b.1.	A resolution of 10 bit or more but less than 12 bit, with an 'adjusted update rate' exceeding 3,500 MSPS; or
3.A.1.a.5.b.2.	A resolution of 12 bit or more and having any of the following:
3.A.1.a.5.b.2.a.	An 'adjusted update rate' exceeding 1,250 MSPS but not exceeding 3,500 MSPS, and having any of the following:
3.A.1.a.5.b.2.a.1.	A settling time less than 9 ns to arrive at or within 0.024% of full scale from a full scale step; or
3.A.1.a.5.b.2.a.2.	A 'Spurious Free Dynamic Range' (SFDR) greater than 68 dBc (carrier) when synthesising a full scale analogue signal of 100 MHz or the highest full scale analogue signal frequency specified below 100 MHz; or
3.A.1.a.5.b.2.b.	An 'adjusted update rate' exceeding 3,500 MSPS;

3.A.1.a.5.b.Technical Notes.1.	'Spurious Free Dynamic Range' (SFDR) is defined as the ratio of the RMS value of the carrier frequency (maximum signal component) at the input of the DAC to the RMS value of the next largest noise or harmonic distortion component at its output.
3.A.1.a.5.b.Technical Notes.2.	SFDR is determined directly from the specification table or from the characterisation plots of SFDR versus frequency.
3.A.1.a.5.b.Technical Notes.3.	A signal is defined to be full scale when its amplitude is greater than -3 dBfs (full scale).
3.A.1.a.5.b.Technical Notes.4.	'Adjusted update rate' for DACs:
3.A.1.a.5.b.Technical Notes.4.a.	For conventional (non-interpolating) DACs, the 'adjusted update rate' is the rate at which the digital signal is converted to an analogue signal and the output analogue values are changed by the DAC.  For DACs where the interpolation mode may be bypassed (interpolation factor of one), the DAC should be considered as a conventional (non-interpolating) DAC.
3.A.1.a.5.b.Technical Notes.4.b.	For interpolating DACs (oversampling DACs), the 'adjusted update rate' is defined as the DAC update rate divided by the smallest interpolating factor. For interpolating DACs, the 'adjusted update rate' may be referred to by different terms including:
3.A.1.a.5.b.Technical Notes.4.b.	• input data rate
3.A.1.a.5.b.Technical Notes.4.b.	• input word rate
3.A.1.a.5.b.Technical Notes.4.b.	• input sample rate
3.A.1.a.5.b.Technical Notes.4.b.	• maximum total input bus rate
3.A.1.a.5.b.Technical Notes.4.b.	• maximum DAC clock rate for DAC clock input.
