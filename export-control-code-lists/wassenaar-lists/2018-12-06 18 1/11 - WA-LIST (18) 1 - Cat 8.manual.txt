
8.A.	SYSTEMS, EQUIPMENT AND COMPONENTS

8.A.1.	Submersible vehicles and surface vessels, as follows:
8.A.1.N.B.	For the status of equipment for submersible vehicles, see:
8.A.1.N.B.	- Category 6 for sensors;
8.A.1.N.B.	- Categories 7 and 8 for navigation equipment;
8.A.1.N.B.	- Category 8.A. for underwater equipment.

8.A.1.a.	Manned, tethered submersible vehicles designed to operate at depths exceeding 1,000 m;

8.A.1.b.	Manned, untethered submersible vehicles having any of the following:
8.A.1.b.1.	Designed to 'operate autonomously' and having a lifting capacity of all the following:
8.A.1.b.1.a.	10% or more of their weight in air; and
8.A.1.b.1.b.	15 kN or more;
8.A.1.b.2.	Designed to operate at depths exceeding 1,000 m; or
8.A.1.b.3.	Having all of the following:
8.A.1.b.3.a.	Designed to continuously 'operate autonomously' for 10 hours or more; and
8.A.1.b.3.b.	'Range' of 25 nautical miles or more; 

8.A.1.b.Technical Notes.1.	For the purposes of 8.A.1.b., 'operate autonomously' means fully submerged, without snorkel, all systems working and cruising at minimum speed at which the submersible can safely control its depth dynamically by using its depth planes only, with no need for a support vessel or support base on the surface, sea-bed or shore, and containing a propulsion system for submerged or surface use.
8.A.1.b.Technical Notes.2.	For the purposes of 8.A.1.b., 'range' means half the maximum distance a submersible vehicle can 'operate autonomously'.

8.A.1.c.	Unmanned submersible vehicles, as follows:
8.A.1.c.1.	Unmanned submersible vehicles having any of the following:
8.A.1.c.1.a.	Designed for deciding a course relative to any geographical reference without real-time human assistance;
8.A.1.c.1.b.	Acoustic data or command link; or
8.A.1.c.1.c.	Optical data or command link exceeding 1,000 m;
8.A.1.c.2.	Unmanned submersible vehicles, not specified in 8.A.1c.1., having all of the following:
8.A.1.c.2.a.	Designed to operate with a tether;
8.A.1.c.2.b.	Designed to operate at depths exceeding 1,000 m; and
8.A.1.c.2.c.	Having any of the following:
8.A.1.c.2.c.1.	Designed for self-propelled manoeuvre using propulsion motors or thrusters specified by 8.A.2.a.2.; or
8.A.1.c.2.c.2.	Fibre optic data link;

8.A.1.d.	Not used since 2018

8.A.1.e.	Ocean salvage systems with a lifting capacity exceeding 5 MN for salvaging objects from depths exceeding 250 m and having any of the following:
8.A.1.e.1.	Dynamic positioning systems capable of position keeping within 20 m of a given point provided by the navigation system; or
8.A.1.e.2.	Seafloor navigation and navigation integration systems, for depths exceeding 1,000 m and with positioning "accuracies" to within 10 m of a predetermined point.

8.A.2.h.	"Robots" specially designed for underwater use, controlled by using a dedicated computer and  having any of the following:
8.A.2.h.1.	Systems that control the "robot" using information from sensors which measure force or torque applied to an external object, distance to an external object, or tactile sense between the "robot" and an external object; or
8.A.2.h.2.	The ability to exert a force of 250 N or more or a torque of 250 Nm or more and using titanium based alloys or "composite" "fibrous or filamentary materials" in their structural members;

8.A.2.i.	Remotely controlled articulated manipulators specially designed or modified for use with submersible vehicles and having any of the following:
8.A.2.i.1.	Systems which control the manipulator using information from sensors which measure any of the following:
8.A.2.i.1.a.	Torque or force applied to an external object; or
8.A.2.i.1.b.	Tactile sense between the manipulator and an external object; or
8.A.2.i.2.	Controlled by proportional master-slave techniques and having 5 degrees of 'freedom of movement' or more;

8.A.2.i.2.Technical Note.	Only functions having proportionally related motion control using positional feedback are counted when determining the number of degrees of 'freedom of movement'.

8.A.2.o.1.	Not used since 2014
8.A.2.o.2.	Water-screw propeller, power generation systems or transmission systems, designed for use on vessels, as follows:
8.A.2.o.2.a.	Controllable-pitch propellers and hub assemblies, rated at more than 30 MW;
8.A.2.o.2.b.	Internally liquid-cooled electric propulsion engines with a power output exceeding 2.5 MW;
8.A.2.o.2.c.	"Superconductive" propulsion engines or permanent magnet electric propulsion engines, with a power output exceeding 0.1 MW;
8.A.2.o.2.d.	Power transmission shaft systems incorporating "composite" material components and capable of transmitting more than 2 MW;

8.A.2.o.3.	Noise reduction systems designed for use on vessels of 1,000 tonnes displacement or more, as follows:
8.A.2.o.3.a.	Systems that attenuate underwater noise at frequencies below 500 Hz and consist of compound acoustic mounts for the acoustic isolation of diesel engines, diesel generator sets, gas turbines, gas turbine generator sets, propulsion motors or propulsion reduction gears, specially designed for sound or vibration isolation and having an intermediate mass exceeding 30% of the equipment to be mounted;

8.A.2.o.3.b.	'Active noise reduction or cancellation systems' or magnetic bearings, specially designed for power transmission systems;

8.A.2.o.3.b.Technical Note.	'Active noise reduction or cancellation systems' incorporate electronic control systems capable of actively reducing equipment vibration by the generation of anti-noise or anti-vibration signals directly to the source.

8.A.2.r.	Diver deterrent acoustic systems specially designed or modified to disrupt divers and having a sound pressure level equal to or exceeding 190 dB (reference 1 µPa at 1 m) at frequencies of 200 Hz and below.
8.A.2.r.Note 1.	8.A.2.r. does not apply to diver deterrent systems based on underwater explosive devices, air guns or combustible sources.
8.A.2.r.Note 2.	8.A.2.r. includes diver deterrent acoustic systems that use spark gap sources, also known as plasma sound sources.
